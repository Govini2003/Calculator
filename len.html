Do You Know?

Meter (m): The base unit of length in the International System of Units (SI). Originally defined in 1793 as one ten-millionth of the distance from the equator to the North Pole.


Kilometer (km): Equals 1,000 meters. Commonly used to measure longer distances, such as the length of roads or the distance between cities.


Centimeter (cm): Equals 0.01 meters. Widely used in everyday measurements, especially in fields like tailoring and interior design.


Millimeter (mm): Equals 0.001 meters. Often used in engineering, manufacturing, and precision measurements.


Micrometer (µm): Equals 1×10⁻⁶ meters. Essential in scientific fields like biology and materials science for measuring microscopic structures.


Nanometer (nm): Equals 1×10⁻⁹ meters. Used primarily in nanotechnology and chemistry to measure atomic and molecular scales.


Mile: Equals approximately 1,609.34 meters. Commonly used in the United States and the United Kingdom for measuring longer distances like road travel.


Light-Year: The distance light travels in one year, about 9.46 trillion kilometers. Used in astronomy to express distances between stars and galaxies.
