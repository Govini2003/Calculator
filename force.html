Newton's First Law:

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at a constant velocity unless acted upon by an external force. This is also known as the law of inertia.

Newton's Second Law:

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on the object and inversely proportional to its mass. This is expressed with the formula F = ma.

Newton's Third Law:

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. This means that any force exerted on a body will result in an equal force exerted in the opposite direction.
